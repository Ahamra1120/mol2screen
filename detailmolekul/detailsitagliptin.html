Sitagliptin

C16H15F6N5O

Sitagliptin

(3R)-3-amino-1-[3-(trifluoromethyl)-6,8-dihydro-5H-[1,2,4]triazolo[4,3-a]pyrazin-7-yl]-4-(2,4,5-trifluorophenyl)butan-1-one

Sitagliptin is an oral prescription medication used to manage type 2 diabetes in adults. It is a dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) inhibitor that helps control blood sugar levels when used in conjunction with a healthy diet and regular exercise.

Sitagliptin works by blocking the enzyme DPP-4, which normally breaks down natural hormones called incretins (GLP-1 and GIP). By inhibiting DPP-4, sitagliptin increases the active levels of these incretin hormones in the body. This leads to:

  • Increased insulin release from the pancreas, especially after meals.
  • Decreased glucagon secretion from the pancreas, which reduces the amount of sugar the liver produces.
  • he effect is glucose-dependent, meaning it primarily works when blood sugar is high, which generally minimizes the risk of low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) when used alone.
