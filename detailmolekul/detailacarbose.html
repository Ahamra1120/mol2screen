Acarbose

(2R,3R,4R,5R)-4-[(2R,3R,4R,5S,6R)-5-[(2R,3R,4S,5S,6R)-3,4-dihydroxy-6-methyl-5-[[(1S,4R,5S,6S)-4,5,6-trihydroxy-3-(hydroxymethyl)cyclohex-2-en-1-yl]amino]oxan-2-yl]oxy-3,4-dihydroxy-6-(hydroxymethyl)oxan-2-yl]oxy-2,3,5,6-tetrahydroxyhexanal

Acarbose is an oral prescription medication used to manage type 2 diabetes. It is an alpha-glucosidase inhibitor that works by slowing the digestion of complex carbohydrates in the intestine, which prevents a sharp increase in blood sugar after meals. It can be used alone or in combination with other diabetes medications like insulin.

  • Enzyme inhibition: It competitively inhibits intestinal enzymes, such as alpha-glucosidase and pancreatic alpha-amylase, that are responsible for breaking down carbohydrates.
  • Delayed absorption: By blocking these enzymes, acarbose delays the digestion and absorption of sugars, which leads to a smaller and more gradual rise in blood glucose levels after eating.
