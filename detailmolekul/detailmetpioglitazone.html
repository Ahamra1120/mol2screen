Pioglitazon

5-[[4-[2-(5-ethyl-2-pyridinyl)ethoxy]phenyl]methyl]-1,3-thiazolidine-2,4-dione

Pioglitazone (brand name examples: Actos, Pionix) is an oral prescription medication used, along with diet and exercise, to manage high blood sugar levels in adults with type 2 diabetes. It is not used for type 1 diabetes or diabetic ketoacidosis.

Metformin, a biguanide, lowers high blood glucose levels through several mechanisms:

  • Pioglitazone belongs to the class of medications called thiazolidinediones. It works by increasing the body's sensitivity and response to insulin in muscle, fat, and liver tissues, helping the body use insulin more effectively to move sugar from the blood into cells for energy.
  • It is typically taken by mouth once daily, with or without food, and should be taken at the same time each day.
