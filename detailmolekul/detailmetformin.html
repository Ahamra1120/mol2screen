Metformin

3–(diaminomethylidene)–1,1–dimethylguanidine

Metformin is a first-line prescription medication for managing type 2 diabetes. It helps control high blood sugar, but is not used for type 1 diabetes. It may also be prescribed off-label for conditions like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

Metformin, a biguanide, lowers high blood glucose levels through several mechanisms:

  • Decreases liver glucose production: It reduces the amount of glucose your liver releases into your blood.
  • Increases insulin sensitivity: It helps your body's cells, particularly in muscles, become more responsive to insulin, allowing for better glucose uptake.
  • Reduces intestinal glucose absorption: It lessens the amount of sugar absorbed from food in your intestines.
