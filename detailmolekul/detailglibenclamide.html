Glibenclamide

5-chloro-N-[2-[4-(cyclohexylcarbamoylsulfamoyl)phenyl]ethyl]-2-methoxybenzamide

Glibenclamide (also known as glyburide) is an oral antidiabetic medication belonging to the sulfonylurea class, used to manage high blood sugar levels in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus. It is used as an adjunct to diet, exercise, and weight reduction when lifestyle changes alone are not enough to control blood glucose.

Glibenclamide works primarily by stimulating the pancreas to produce and release more insulin. It achieves this by binding to and closing ATP-sensitive potassium channels (KATP) on the pancreatic beta cells, which causes the cell membrane to depolarize. This depolarization opens voltage-dependent calcium channels, increasing intracellular calcium concentrations and triggering the secretion of insulin.
