About Us

We are a team of five students from MAN 13 Jakarta who are passionate about addressing challenges in chemistry and health education. Mol2Screen is designed to be responsive, informative, and user-friendly. Functional and field tests demonstrated that the system performed well and achieved an average user satisfaction score of 4.78 out of 5, categorized as "Very Good". This confirms that the platform is suitable as a modern and interactive supplementary learning medium.

Our Vision and Motivation

This project was initiated in response to several key challenges in learning new chemical compounds:

1. Lack of Interactive Resources: Students face limited learning resources and lack of interactive barriers when learning about new chemical compounds.

2. Difficulty in Visualization: Students often struggle with abstract chemistry concepts and have low spatial ability, particularly in visualizing intermolecular interactions at the submicroscopic level.

3. Need for Innovation: There is a need for innovative, visual, and interactive learning media to understand the structure, properties, and therapeutic potential of new bioactive compounds.

Our vision is to create an accessible learning platform that not only solves social-educational problems but also honors and supports the nation’s inventiveness.

Mol2Screen

We developed Mol2Screen, a web-based digital learning platform focusing on chemical compounds as diabetes therapy. The platform was developed using the Waterfall development model.

Mol2Screen is an educational project designed to promote scientific understanding. The information presented is not meant for medical or commercial use.

Key Features

  • Juanleoxy Fahrulanoside Molecule: The platform delivers scientific material regarding the compound Juanleoxy Fahrulanoside (C₁₂H₂₃NO₉), a new Indonesian compound identified as a potential candidate for diabetes mellitus therapy.
  • Interactive 3D Molecular Visualization: A module that allows users to rotate, zoom, and analyze the 3D molecular structure of Juanleoxy Fahrulanoside, which was developed using Three.js.
  • Content Page: Presents material in a literary and visual format, aimed at helping users understand the molecular structure and its GLP-1 modulator mechanism in diabetes therapy.
  • Games Page/Quiz Module: An interactive module featuring multiple-choice questions to assess the user’s understanding of the compounds, their function, and how they work.
